About the Program

The Department of Computer Science at UP Cebu has been recognized as a Center of Excellence in Information Technology since 2007. The BS Computer Science curriculum builds from mathematical and theoretical foundations to core CS professional skills, culminating in emerging technologies such as AI, Machine Learning, and Data Analytics.

This course is part of the department's commitment to producing competitive, innovative, and responsible leaders in computer science. Research activities are supported by the department's Center for Research in Intelligent Systems.

Course Description

This course introduces the principles and techniques of data analytics with emphasis on real-world applications in the Philippine context. Students will learn to collect, clean, explore, visualize, and model data to extract actionable insights for decision-making.

Topics include statistical foundations, data wrangling, exploratory data analysis, visualization principles, regression and classification, clustering, time series analysis, and text analytics. Ethical considerations in data science are integrated throughout, including the Philippine Data Privacy Act (RA 10173).

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

  1. Apply statistical concepts to analyze and interpret data
  2. Clean, transform, and prepare data for analysis
  3. Conduct exploratory data analysis and create effective visualizations
  4. Build and evaluate predictive models (regression, classification, clustering)
  5. Analyze time series data and perform basic forecasting
  6. Apply text analytics techniques for unstructured data
  7. Communicate data insights through storytelling and dashboards
  8. Apply ethical principles in data collection, analysis, and reporting

Laboratory Exercises

Weekly hands-on labs using Philippine datasets. Labs are submitted as Jupyter Notebooks. You are expected to spend 2-3 hours per lab outside of class time.

Sample lab topics:

  • Lab 1: Python & pandas basics with PSA population data
  • Lab 4: EDA on Philippine tourism arrivals
  • Lab 7: Predicting house prices in Metro Manila
  • Lab 10: Forecasting OFW remittances with ARIMA
Submission Policy: Labs are due by 11:59 PM on the specified deadline. Late submissions receive a 10% deduction per day. No submissions accepted more than 3 days late.

Capstone Project

An end-to-end analytics project using Philippine data that demonstrates your ability to collect, clean, analyze, model, and visualize data. Projects are done in groups of 2-3 students.

Course Policies

Attendance

Honor Code

As a student of the University of the Philippines, you are expected to uphold the highest standards of academic integrity. All submitted work must represent your own understanding and effort.

AI Use Policy

AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, etc.) are allowed in this course, but must be used responsibly. AI is a powerful tool for learning and productivity—learning to use it effectively is a valuable skill for data analysts.

Responsible AI use:

  • Use AI to understand concepts, not to bypass learning
  • Use AI to debug and improve code you've written
  • Use AI to explore alternative approaches and learn best practices
  • Always verify AI outputs—they can be wrong or outdated
  • Cite your AI usage in your submissions (e.g., "Used Claude to debug my pandas code")

Not acceptable:

  • Submitting AI-generated work without understanding it
  • Using AI during exams or quizzes (unless explicitly allowed)
  • Copying AI outputs verbatim without verification or attribution
  • Using AI to complete work for others
Why this policy? In the real world, you'll use AI tools daily. The goal is to learn with AI, not to have AI learn for you. If you can't explain your code or analysis, you haven't learned.

Academic Integrity

Violations of academic integrity will result in disciplinary action per UP rules:

  • Plagiarism (copying without attribution)
  • Cheating on exams or quizzes
  • Submitting others' work as your own
  • Fabricating data or results

Communication

Course announcements, materials, and submissions will be handled through the designated course platform (to be announced at the start of the semester). For questions, use the class group chat or email me directly. I will respond to emails within 48 hours on weekdays.

Data Privacy & Ethics

This course adheres to RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012). As a data analyst, you have responsibilities to:

  • Use only publicly available or properly anonymized datasets
  • Never collect personal data without consent
  • Report findings responsibly and avoid causing harm
  • Acknowledge data sources and limitations
  • Consider potential biases in your data and models
